Government Classification Levels

Top Secret "Exceptionally Grave Damage"

Unauthorized disclosure could cause exceptionally grave damage to national security.

Examples

  • Intelligence sources and methods
  • Nuclear weapon designs
  • Military operations plans
  • Cryptographic keys
Secret "Serious Damage"

Unauthorized disclosure could cause serious damage to national security.

Examples

  • Military deployment plans
  • Intelligence reports
  • Weapons system details
  • Diplomatic cables
Confidential "Damage"

Unauthorized disclosure could cause damage to national security.

Examples

  • Routine military info
  • Personnel records
  • Training materials
  • Technical manuals
Unclassified "Public"

Information that is not sensitive and can be freely shared.

Examples

  • Press releases
  • Public reports
  • Marketing materials
  • Open-source info

Commercial Classification Levels

Confidential

Highest sensitivity - restricted to specific individuals. Breach causes severe damage.

Examples

  • M&A plans
  • Trade secrets
  • Executive communications
  • Security credentials
Private

Personal information requiring protection. Regulated by privacy laws.

Examples

  • PII (SSN, DOB)
  • PHI (medical records)
  • Financial data
  • HR records
Sensitive

Internal use only. Disclosure could negatively impact operations.

Examples

  • Internal memos
  • Project plans
  • Vendor contracts
  • Process documents
Public

Approved for external sharing. No impact from disclosure.

Examples

  • Marketing materials
  • Job postings
  • Product brochures
  • Annual reports

üìå Special Data Types

PII (Personally Identifiable Information)

Any data that can identify an individual: SSN, name+address, biometrics. Protected by NIST SP 800-122.

PHI (Protected Health Information)

Health data linked to individuals. Protected by HIPAA. Includes diagnoses, treatment, insurance info.

Proprietary Data

Trade secrets, source code, formulas - data giving competitive advantage. Loss impacts business value.
