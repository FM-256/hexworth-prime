üéØ Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
Artifacts indicating potential intrusion or malicious activity
  • File hashes (MD5, SHA1, SHA256)
  • IP addresses and domain names
  • URLs and email addresses
  • Registry entries and file paths
  • Mutex names and process names
  • Network signatures and patterns

  • Business-critical asset monitoring
  • Active defense & honeypots
  • Canary tokens for detection
  • LOLBins (Living off the Land)

Vulnerability Prioritization Factors
‚ö° Exploitability
Likelihood and ease of exploitation
  • Public exploit available (Metasploit, ExploitDB)
  • Active exploitation in the wild
  • Attack complexity (network vs local)
  • Privileges required for exploitation
  • User interaction requirements
üè¢ Asset Criticality
Business impact and value of affected systems
  • Data sensitivity classification
  • Business process dependency
  • Regulatory compliance requirements
  • Internet-facing vs internal
  • Revenue impact potential

  • Network segmentation
  • IPS/IDS signatures
  • WAF rules
  • Access control restrictions
  • Monitoring and alerting

Incident Response Phases (NIST)
üìã 1. Preparation
Building IR capability before incidents occur
  • IR plan and playbooks
  • Team training and exercises
  • Tool deployment (EDR, SIEM, forensics)
  • Communication channels
  • Legal and compliance coordination
üîç 2. Detection & Analysis
Identifying and investigating security events
  • Alert triage and validation
  • IOC identification
  • Scope determination
  • Timeline reconstruction
  • Evidence preservation
üõë 3. Containment
Limiting the impact of the incident
  • Short-term: Isolate affected systems
  • Long-term: Remediate vulnerabilities
  • Network segmentation
  • Account lockdown
  • Block malicious IPs/domains
üîß 4. Eradication & Recovery
Removing threats and restoring operations
  • Malware removal
  • Patch vulnerable systems
  • Restore from clean backups
  • Credential resets
  • Validate system integrity
üìä 5. Post-Incident
Learning and improving from the incident
  • Root cause analysis
  • Lessons learned meeting
  • Update playbooks and procedures
  • Improve detection capabilities
  • Executive reporting
