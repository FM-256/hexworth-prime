Risk Categories

Understanding the impact levels of risk

LOW
  • > Limited impact on company
  • > Financial loss is sustainable
  • > Short-term process disruption
  • > Short-term recovery
  • > No personal injuries
MODERATE
  • > Serious impact on company
  • > Substantial but sustainable loss
  • > Long process disruption
  • > Substantial recovery time
  • > May involve personal injury
HIGH
  • > Severe/catastrophic impact
  • > Business may cease to exist
  • > Very unlikely to recover
  • > Could involve loss of life
  • > Complete system compromise

📊 Risk Analysis Approaches

Quantitative Analysis

  • Based on financial figures
  • Provides financial basis for decisions
  • Objective and measurable
  • More accurate but complex
  • Requires more data to collect
ALE = SLE x ARO

Annualized Loss Expectancy = Single Loss Expectancy x Annual Rate of Occurrence

Qualitative Analysis

  • Subjective and quick to implement
  • Identifies threats and likelihood
  • Probability/impact ranking matrix
  • Classifies risks by source/effect
  • Less data required

🛡 Risk Response Strategies

🔹 Mitigate

Invest in controls to decrease risk. Example: firewalls, encryption, access controls.

🚫 Avoid

Don't implement what creates the risk. Example: don't use vulnerable technology.

✅ Accept

Acknowledge risk exists but don't control it. Example: natural disaster risk with DR plan.

🔀 Transfer

Have someone else take the risk. Examples: cyber insurance, cloud services.

⚠ NEVER ignore the risk!

📉 Residual Risk

After completing risk response, reassess company risk levels. Risk is never fully removed or mitigated to zero. The goal is to have residual risk within company risk tolerance levels. Once acceptable, proceed to operations. Risk must be continuously monitored as new threats and vulnerabilities introduce new risks.

💡 Key Takeaways

✓ Risk = Asset + Vulnerability + Threat
✓ Risk process: Frame > Assess > Respond > Monitor
✓ NIST RMF 800-37 provides 7-step framework
✓ Quantitative uses ALE = SLE x ARO formula
✓ Four responses: Mitigate, Avoid, Accept, Transfer
✓ Residual risk must be within tolerance levels
