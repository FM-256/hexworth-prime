‚ö° Laser Printing Process

The laser printing process uses 7 steps to fuse toner onto paper. This is a key A+ exam topic!

1
Processing - Printer receives and processes print data
2
Charging - Drum receives uniform negative charge (-600V)
3
Exposing - Laser writes image, discharging areas to ~-100V
4
Developing - Negatively charged toner attracts to exposed areas
5
Transferring - Positive charge pulls toner from drum to paper
6
Fusing - Heat (200¬∞C) and pressure bond toner to paper
7
Cleaning - Blade removes excess toner, erase lamp neutralizes drum
üí° A+ Exam Tip: Memorize the 7 steps! Common mnemonic: "People Can Easily Develop Through Frequent Classes" (Processing, Charging, Exposing, Developing, Transferring, Fusing, Cleaning)

üå°Ô∏è Thermal Printing

Direct Thermal

  • Uses heat-sensitive paper
  • No ink or toner needed
  • Prints fade over time
  • Used for: Receipts, shipping labels

Thermal Transfer

  • Uses wax/resin ribbon
  • More durable prints
  • Higher quality barcodes
  • Used for: Asset tags, product labels
