Risk Categories

Understanding the impact levels of cloud security risks

Low Risk

  • Limited impact on company and personnel
  • Financial loss is sustainable
  • Short-term loss of processes
  • Short-term recovery
  • No personal injuries

Moderate Risk

  • Serious impact on company and resources
  • Substantial but sustainable financial loss
  • Long-term loss of processes
  • Substantial recovery time
  • May involve personal injury

High Risk

  • Catastrophic impact to company
  • Business may cease to exist
  • Very unlikely to recover
  • Could involve loss of life
  • Immediate action required

Security Controls & Countermeasures

Defense in depth - one level of protection is not enough

Essential Cloud Security Controls

üîê Identity & Access Management

Ensures only authorized users and devices can access cloud resources. Includes user accounts, roles, permissions, and authentication methods.

üîí Data Encryption

Protects confidentiality and integrity using cryptographic algorithms. Apply at rest, in transit, and in use.

üõ°Ô∏è Firewall & Network Security

Regulates traffic between cloud resources and networks. Configure rules and policies to allow or deny traffic.

üìä Security Monitoring & Auditing

Enables detection and response of security incidents. Collect, analyze, and report on security logs and events.

üîë Multi-Factor Authentication

Adds extra layer of security requiring two or more forms of identification (password + fingerprint, etc.).

üíæ Regular Backups & DR Plan

Ensures data can be recovered quickly. Includes procedures for backup, restoration, and failover.

Risk Analysis Methods

Quantitative vs Qualitative approaches

Quantitative Risk Analysis

Objective analysis based on figures that impact the company financially. Provides a financial basis for risk treatment decisions.

ALE = SLE √ó ARO
  • ALE: Annualized Loss Expectancy
  • SLE: Single Loss Expectancy
  • ARO: Annualized Rate of Occurrence
  • More accurate but time-consuming
  • Requires measurable data
  • Provides go/no-go decisions

Qualitative Risk Analysis

Subjective and quick to implement. Uses probability/impact ranking matrix to prioritize risks.

  • Identifies potential threats and opportunities
  • Assesses likelihood and impact
  • Classifies risks by source or effect
  • Quick and cost-effective
  • Good for initial assessment
  • Uses High/Medium/Low ratings
