🔐 Encryption Techniques

📦 Encryption at Rest

  • Encrypts data stored in databases or file systems
  • Data is encrypted, rendering it unreadable in plaintext
  • Keys are stored securely for authorized access only
  • Uses access controls and regular backups
  • AES-256 is the widely adopted standard

🚀 Encryption in Transit

  • Secures data traveling between sender and receiver
  • TLS encrypts data during network transit
  • Prevents exposure to unauthorized entities
  • Digital signatures verify message integrity
  • Protects against confidentiality attacks

🔑 Key Management

Cloud Provider Managed Keys

  • > AES-256 encryption at rest by default
  • > Automatic key rotation (often more frequent than annually)
  • > Storage Service Encryption enabled by default
  • > Lower management overhead

Customer Managed Keys

  • > Required for Separation of Duties (SoD) compliance
  • > Full control over key lifecycle
  • > Meet regulatory and legal standards
  • > Higher management responsibility

💾 Backup vs. Replication

Backup

  • Saves a copy of the data at point-in-time
  • Aligned with Recovery Point Objective (RPO)
  • Combination of full and incremental backups
  • Protects against ransomware attacks
  • High RPO - data from recent backup

Replication

  • Synchronizes data across multiple systems
  • Enhances resiliency and redundancy
  • Low Recovery Time Objective (RTO)
  • If data corrupts, all replicas corrupt
  • Used for disaster recovery hot sites

Best Practice: Replication + Backup together provide resilient infrastructure with low RTO and high RPO.

💡 Key Takeaways

✓ Know, Protect, Prevent, Govern - the four pillars of data security
✓ Encrypt data at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS)
✓ Choose customer-managed keys for compliance requirements
✓ DLP prevents intentional and unintentional data exposure
✓ Backup protects data; Replication ensures availability
✓ Combine backup + replication for comprehensive DR strategy
